# Dossier template — `workspace/research/YYYY-MM-DD-dossier-<kebab-name>.md`

Every section appears in every dossier; thin sections say they're thin rather than disappearing. Claim markers — (self-reported), (single source), (unverified), (inference), (contested), (secondary only), `[NO DATA FOUND]` — are the same vocabulary as the rest of the Operator Library and travel with every claim. Plain prose, no voice profile — internal working document. Present it however relays the information best — tables, charts, visuals, plain prose: whatever you judge serves the reader.

```markdown
---
title: "<Name> — Dossier"
type: dossier
date: <YYYY-MM-DD>
subject: person | company
occasion: "<the interaction this serves, e.g. discovery call 2026-06-14>"
---

# <Name>

**As of <YYYY-MM-DD>.** Roles, numbers, and offerings below are snapshots —
re-verify the load-bearing ones if the meeting is more than a few weeks out.

## Identity
<who this is — and the **anchors**: the 2+ independent
matching facts that pin this as the right person/company (e.g. "site bio +
conference speaker page agree: <role> at <company>, <location>"). If any
same-name confusion was possible, note how it was ruled out. Anything that
couldn't be confidently attached goes under "Possibly the same person
(unconfirmed)" at the bottom — never merged in.>

## What they do now
<current role/business, dated and sourced. For companies: what they sell,
to whom, pricing if public, team size signals, what their hiring suggests
they're building. Self-descriptions marked (self-reported) until confirmed.>

## Track record
<the verifiable history that bears on the occasion: ventures, outcomes,
published work, prior roles — each dated, sourced, marked. Patterns stated
as facts with evidence, never as character verdicts.>

## Their angle
<what they appear to want, optimize for, and say publicly — their stated
positions, recurring themes in talks/posts, who they associate with
professionally. Quote sparingly with anchors; (inference) marked as such.>

## Mutual context
<where their world touches the operator's: shared audience, adjacent offers,
common platforms or people, anything from context/business.md and
context/ICP.md that frames this relationship. "None found" is a valid answer.>

## Prep for <the occasion>
<written from THIS operator's offer, positioning, and goals — from the
context profiles — never generic:>
- **Talking points:** <where the operator's strengths meet this
  subject's situation>
- **Questions to ask:** <the things the public record can't answer
  and the decision needs>
- **Watchouts:** <gaps, contradictions, or incentives to keep in
  view, each tied to evidence above>

## Could not verify
<load-bearing claims that resisted confirmation, each with what was tried.
Empty is fine; absent is not.>

## Source table
| Source | Type | Date | Used for | Quality note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| <linked name> | primary / official / practitioner / secondary | <pub date> | <which sections> | <named author? lateral-checked? (self-reported) origin?> |
```

**Filing notes**

- One dossier file per subject per run; a fresh meeting months later gets a fresh dated file (the old one stays as the before-picture). Sharpen the slug rather than overwrite.
- If a subject later becomes a tracked competitor, point the operator to the Competitor Tracker plugin and link this dossier from there — don't maintain two living records.
- Corrections after the fact: prepend `> **CORRECTION (<YYYY-MM-DD>):** <what was wrong, what's true, how verified>` and leave the body intact.
