# Voice Interview Prompts

Use the question set matching the subject type. 4–6 questions max. Skip questions that don't fit. Don't read verbatim — paraphrase, conversational.

---

## Self — the operator's own voice

1. What do you notice about how you write? Anything you'd point to as distinctly you?
2. Anything you intentionally avoid — words, phrases, patterns you hate seeing under your name?
3. Which pieces of your past writing are you proudest of? What made them work?
4. Where does your voice feel most authentic — newsletters, social posts, talks, somewhere else?
5. How different is your writing voice from your talking voice? Are we bridging that gap or keeping them separate?
6. Any AI patterns you actively hate when you spot them in other people's writing?

## Client — a specific person

1. Whose voice exactly — the person across everything, or the person in one specific lane (their newsletter, their LinkedIn)?
2. Does the client have an explicit style guide or voice doc? Where can I read it?
3. Any words or phrases the client refuses to use? Any they use constantly that must be preserved?
4. Where does their voice live most clearly — their best pieces, their talks, their interviews?
5. What does the "off" version of their voice look like — content that didn't sound like them, and why?
6. What's the client's bar — have they rejected drafts before? What was wrong with those?

## Brand — a house voice

1. Is this brand voice anchored to a person (a founder whose voice the brand borrows) or genuinely impersonal?
2. Is there a style guide or brand book? Where? (It's stated intent — the published pieces are the evidence; flag mismatches.)
3. Which 2–3 published pieces does the team consider the reference — "this is exactly our voice"?
4. Any banned words or phrases? Any required ones (product naming, capitalization, terms of art)?
5. Who is the brand talking to, and what's the relationship — peer, guide, vendor?
6. Which competitor or neighbor voices must this brand NOT be confused with?

---

## Pacing rules

- **Paraphrase, don't recite.** Conversational beats formal.
- **Probe thin answers once.** "Tell me more about that." "What did that look like?"
- **Skip the irrelevant.** No style guide? Don't push for one.
- **Take notes in real time.** Answers feed the profile directly — especially the avoid-lists and the "off version" descriptions, which become the AI-tells and bad examples.
- **Let answers breathe.** This interview is where the profile gets its judgment calls; the samples provide the evidence.
