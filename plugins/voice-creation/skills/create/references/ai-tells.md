# AI Tells — The Blocklist

The words, phrases, and patterns readers actually complain about as "sounds like AI." This file ships inside every voice profile as its standing blocklist. Compiled June 2026 from large-scale studies (15M biomedical abstracts, 328k analyzed ChatGPT conversations, peer-review corpora), Wikipedia's AI-cleanup field guide, and thousands of real reader/editor complaints.

## How to use this list (read first — this part matters most)

1. **Clusters convict, single hits don't.** Expert AI-spotters work off *clusters* of tells, never one word. One "robust" is fine; "robust" + "seamless" + "It's not just X, it's Y" + four em-dashes in one paragraph is the tell.
2. **The voice profile's personal exceptions override this list.** If the human genuinely uses a word here (proven by a quote from their samples), it's THEIR word — keep it. A blocklist that flattens a real voice has failed.
3. **Don't over-correct.** Sanding out every irregularity pushes writing *toward* the AI statistical profile. Uneven sentence lengths, a little disfluency, an opinion — that's what reads human. Polish is not the goal; the person is.
4. **Tells decay.** "Delve" peaked in 2024 and is already fading while "core" and "modern" rise. Treat this list as current practice, not eternal law.

## 1. The infamous words (the ones with studies and headlines behind them)

delve / delving · tapestry ("rich tapestry of...") · testament ("a testament to") · underscore(s) · showcase / showcasing · intricate / intricacies · pivotal · crucial · landscape (abstract: "the evolving landscape") · meticulous / meticulously · leverage (as a verb) · foster / fostering · boasts (meaning "has") · garner(ed) · realm ("in the realm of") · vibrant · journey (metaphorical) · robust · seamless(ly) ("seamless integration") · multifaceted · "dive into" / "deep dive" / "let's dive in"

## 2. The data-verified second tier

emphasize / emphasizing · highlight / highlighting (as the verb gluing claims together) · enhance / enhancing · align(s) with · bolster(ed) · interplay · enduring ("enduring legacy") · commendable · groundbreaking · surpass(ing) · advancements · notable / noteworthy / notably · innovative · invaluable / "valuable insights" · versatile · profound · vital ("plays a vital role") · ensure / ensuring · aptly / undoubtedly / undeniably · nuanced / nuances · key (as the default adjective) · core · modern (the rising tells as old ones fade)

## 3. The atmospheric words ("nobody talks like this")

nestled ("nestled in the heart of") · bustling · beacon · symphony (as metaphor) · labyrinth · kaleidoscope · whispering / gossamer / crucible / odyssey / zenith · elevate ("elevate your...") · empower(ment) · unlock / unleash ("unlock the potential") · harness · streamline · revolutionize ("revolutionize the way") · cutting-edge · transformative / game-changing · utilize (for "use") · facilitate · paradigm · ever-evolving

## 4. Stock phrases

**Openers:** "In today's fast-paced world / digital age / ever-evolving landscape" (the canonical AI opener) · "I hope this email finds you well" · "Have you ever wondered..." (cliché even pre-AI) · meta-narration ("In this article, we will explore...")

**Frames:** "navigate the complexities/landscape of" · "embark on a journey" · "in the world of..." / "in the heart of" · "when it comes to" · "a deeper understanding of" · "stands as / serves as" (instead of "is") · "plays a crucial/vital/significant role in" · "rich cultural heritage" / "diverse array" · "hustle and bustle" · "game-changer"

**Hedges and throat-clearing:** "It's important to note (that)" · "It's worth noting that" · "That being said" · "At its core" · "To put it simply" · "First and foremost" · "Without further ado" · hedge stacking ("could potentially," "may eventually," "generally speaking" + "typically" + "tends to" in one passage) · vague attribution ("Experts argue," "Industry reports suggest," "widely regarded as") · "shed light on"

**Transitions:** sentence-initial "Additionally," / "Furthermore," / "Moreover," / "Consequently," / "Subsequently," / "Notably," / "Ultimately," — the *density* of formal transitions is the tell; humans just say "Also" or nothing

**Closers:** "In conclusion," / "In summary," / "To sum up" · "At the end of the day" · "Only time will tell" · "The future looks bright" · "As we move forward" · the despite-challenges formula ("Despite these challenges, X continues to thrive") · multiple-endings syndrome (when in doubt, delete the last paragraph)

## 5. Structures and rhythm (above the word level)

- **"It's not just X, it's Y"** — the #1 structural tell of 2025–26; appeared in 6% of ALL ChatGPT conversations in one analysis. Writers have abandoned the device en masse.
- **The staccato negation triplet** — "Not hate. Not anger. Just silence." Reads as AI slop to modern readers even when a human wrote it.
- **"Not only X, but also Y"** everywhere — contrived balance-signaling.
- **Rule of three on autopilot** — adjective, adjective, adjective in every sentence. Classical rhetoric, but AI does it relentlessly; cap it.
- **Em-dash density** — "the ChatGPT hyphen." Presence is fine (humans have used it for centuries); several per paragraph is the tell.
- **Trailing "-ing" analysis** bolted onto sentence ends — "...highlighting the importance of," "...reflecting broader trends."
- **Hedge-then-assert fake balance** — "While X is impressive, Y remains a challenge" — sounds balanced without weighing anything.
- **Synonym cycling** — never repeating a word (the firm → the company → the organization → the entity). Repeating a word is human.
- **Uniform paragraph rhythm** — every paragraph the same 3–5 sentence block, every sentence 15–25 words. Vary or die.
- **Bullet-everything** — bulleting *claims and arguments* instead of writing them. (Real list content — specs, steps, data — should stay lists.)
- **The bold-label bullet** — "**Key Point:** explanation." And mechanical bolding of key terms throughout prose.
- **Emoji-bullet headers** — 🚀💡🔥 per line; readers now audit emoji placement to spot AI posts.
- **Title-Case Headers Everywhere** · a header every 200 words.
- **Rhetorical-question transitions** — "So why does this matter?" "But what does this mean for you?"
- **Infomercial hooks** — "Here's the kicker," "The catch?", "Here's the truth."
- **Relentless positivity / both-sides-ism** — no opinions, no edges, everything "exciting." Humans commit.

## 6. Dead giveaways (chatbot leakage — these end careers)

"As an AI language model..." · "As of my last knowledge update..." · "While specific details are limited..." · paragraph-initial "Certainly!" / "Of course!" / "Absolutely!" · "Great question!" / sycophantic openers · "I hope this helps" · "Would you like me to..." · leftover prompt scaffolding. Any of these in published text is conclusive — search for them before anything ships.

## 7. False positives — do NOT "fix" these out of a real voice

1. **The em dash itself** — centuries of human use; there's an organized backlash defending it. Density is the signal, never presence.
2. **Rule of three and "not X, but Y"** — classical rhetoric humans genuinely use; the profile's quoted examples decide, not this list.
3. **Clean, formal prose** — "too polished" is not proof of AI; real professionals get falsely accused constantly.
4. **Non-native and neurodivergent writing patterns** — systematically over-flagged by detectors (up to 61% false-positive rates in studies). Never "correct" someone's natural register.
5. **Legitimate lists** — changelogs, steps, ingredients belong in bullets.
6. **Repeated favorite words** — a human leaning on a pet word is voice, not a tell.

**The standing rule:** this list bans the *default*. The voice profile's quoted, evidenced patterns always win over this document.
