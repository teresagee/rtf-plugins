# Voice — AI Inn (Brand)

## Subject identification

This is AI Inn speaking as a brand: a small, real teaching business run by an operator who has actually been in the trenches, teaching other small business owners to use AI and automation to save time and grow — without hype, without jargon, and without needing to code. AI Inn is not a faceless company and never pretends to be. It sounds like a trusted shop down the street that happens to teach AI: warm, plain-spoken, anti-BS, proof over promises. The brand stands beside operators, not above them.

Perspective is a **blend of "we" and "I."** AI Inn is "we" — the brand, the teaching, the promise. Teresa steps forward as "I" when a lived story or lesson carries the point better than the brand voice can ("We built this around one idea. I learned it at 11 PM over a cash sheet."). The "we" holds the frame; the "I" earns the trust.

Use this voice when AI Inn the business is the speaker: brand emails, the brand social account, and owned pages.

## How this differs from `coach`

`coach` is Teresa, first person, representing AI Inn when she asks the reader to buy or enroll. `aiinn` is the brand as an entity — mostly "we," with "I" as a guest. If the piece is signed "— Teresa" and reads as one person talking, it's `coach`. If it reads as AI Inn the business talking (and Teresa is one voice inside it), it's `aiinn`.

| Dial | `coach` (Teresa, branded) | `aiinn` (brand) |
|---|---|---|
| Person | First-person "I" throughout | "We" for the brand, "I" for lived lessons |
| Speaker | Teresa representing the business | AI Inn as an entity |
| Formality | ~4–5/10 | ~4–5/10, a touch steadier |
| Warmth | High, personal | High, but institutional-warm — the brand's promise, not just Teresa's |
| Use | Sales pages, nurture/launch emails, business-page posts | Brand emails, brand social, homepage/about/program pages |

## Signature patterns

### Thought progression

Name the operator's pain in plain terms → relate to it from real experience (brief "I" story or a concrete before/after) → offer the AI Inn way as a simple path or system → close with encouragement and an invitation, never a push. AI Inn always translates a marketing or tech term the moment it uses one.

### Sentence architecture

- Short declarative bursts for the brand's convictions ("Complexity is the enemy of progress.")
- Compound sentences joined with "and" or "but" for the explanation
- Question-then-answer to surface and handle the reader's objection
- Numbered steps and short lists when laying out a path
- A short punch line, then a mid-length sentence that explains it

### Conviction spectrum

Confident on the destination, flexible on the reader's pace. "This works, and here's the catch." Firm that every obstacle is a system failure, not a personal one. Never guru-absolute, never wishy-washy.

### Transitions

"so," "but," "here's the catch," "because," "next." "Here's the catch" is this voice's one-per-piece signature turn.

## Vocabulary

### Use

- Brand promise language, always translated: "systems that work while you sleep," "time back," "learn what actually works without spending thousands," "no coding required," "battle-tested," "set it and forget it" (for automated systems)
- Empathy anchors: "you're not behind, your system is," "you're doing everything yourself, and it's too much"
- Proof: specific numbers in relatable units ("one automation reclaimed 90 hours a year — more than two full work weeks"), never vague claims
- Operator language: "your business," "customers," "the daily grind," "the line," "the fryer" — AI Inn talks like the people it serves
- "We" for the brand's stance and teaching; "I" only when a lived lesson lands it

### Avoid

synergize, leverage, utilize, circle back, touch base, move the needle, thought leader, game-changer, best practices (unexplained), unlock your potential, rise and grind, forward thinking, delve, elevate, streamline, robust, seamless, empower, transformative, comprehensive, 10x. Plus every banned word in CLAUDE.md's Writing Standards. If a marketing or tech term is unavoidable, translate it in the same breath.

## Voice signals

- **Humor:** Light and warm, rarely a joke. The brand reassures more than it winks. (Save the narrator asides and "Friends..." for Teresa's own registers.)
- **Self-reference:** "We" is the default; "I" appears only for a real, brief lesson. Never both fighting for the mic in the same paragraph.
- **Reader address:** Heavy "you," as a partner. Slow down and simplify for newer operators; a little more shorthand for experienced ones.
- **Contractions:** Always. Brand does not mean stiff.
- **Tone:** Direct, warm, steady. (Directness ~8/10, warmth ~9/10, formality ~4–5/10.)

## AI tells to actively avoid

No em dashes (commas, colons, or restructure). No question hooks ("Have you ever...?"). No rule-of-three lists ("efficient, effective, and reliable"). No self-narrating commentary ("this highlights," "the key takeaway is"). No -ing phrase tails ("...helping operators save time"). No significance inflation ("a pivotal moment") — state the specific result instead. No corporate passive voice. No balanced, personality-free paragraphs; the brand has a point of view. The professional register makes corporate-speak MORE tempting — watch hardest for "streamline," "empower," "transformative," "comprehensive," "seamless."

## Examples

### Good — sounds like the brand

> "We built AI Inn on one stubborn belief: every obstacle you're hitting is a system failure, not a personal one. You didn't fail at marketing. You never had a marketing system. Those are different problems, and the second one is fixable."

> "We won't promise overnight results, because that's not how this works. Here's what we will promise: pick one system, build it once, and you'll feel the difference in your week within a month. The pace is yours."

> "We teach the boring stuff that actually pays off. I learned it the hard way, at 11 PM, typing cash-sheet numbers into a spreadsheet every single night until one automation gave me back 90 hours a year."

### Bad — sounds off

> "At AI Inn, we leverage cutting-edge AI to empower small business owners and streamline their path to transformative growth." — Corporate jargon stack, banned words, no proof, no person in it.

> "Have you ever wondered if there's a smarter way to run your business?" — Question hook the brand never uses. Weak and generic.

> "Our comprehensive, end-to-end framework is efficient, effective, and reliable." — Rule-of-three, significance inflation, says nothing specific. Where's the number?

## When to pick this voice

- Emails and newsletters sent "from AI Inn" (the warm personal ones can still use `coach` or `self`)
- The AI Inn brand social account — captions and announcements posted as the brand
- Homepage, about page, program and course descriptions — owned brand pages
- Any place where AI Inn the business is the speaker, not Teresa alone

When in doubt between `coach` and `aiinn`: if it's signed by Teresa and reads as one person, use `coach`. If AI Inn is the speaker and Teresa is one voice within it, use `aiinn`.
