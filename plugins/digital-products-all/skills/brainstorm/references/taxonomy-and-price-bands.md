# Product-type taxonomy + price bands (mid-2026)

Shared reference — the brainstorm and scope skills both carry this file so the two ends of the pipeline speak the same vocabulary. The bands are **observed pricing across named solo-operator practitioners' public offers as of mid-2026**, not rules. Several figures are self-reported by the practitioners; treat all of them as orientation, never as a promise to the operator. Re-verify any platform fee before the operator builds a checkout on it.

---

## The taxonomy

| Type | Observed band | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tiny fast-win asset (checklist, swipe file, script pack, short ebook) | $9–$47 ($27 is the canonical funnel front-end) | Fastest to ship; proven as the entry rung of ascension funnels (Cole/Bush's published funnel math; self-reported) |
| Operating template / system / workspace OS | $27–$150 | The strongest-selling category in the 2025→2026 shift toward deployable systems; Welsh's $150 "OS" products anchor the top of the band |
| Prompt library / AI toolkit | $50–$150 one-off, or delivered as paid-newsletter drops (~$20/mo) | The "products as paid posts inside a subscription" model has at least one public ~$400k/yr proof (self-reported, Write With AI) |
| Mini-course / workshop (90-min or 7-day) | $50–$300 | The implementation-first education lane — short, outcome-shaped, not a video library |
| Flagship course | $150 (cheap-volume pole) to ~$900 (premium-depth pole) | Two viable poles, not a spectrum to split the difference on — see pricing architecture in the scope skill's validation reference |
| Paid newsletter | $10–$20/mo | Works when product drops live inside it |
| Community / membership | $20–$99/mo (most $20–$50) | A real product with its own operations load, not a bolt-on |
| Cohort course / challenge | $375–$800 | Doubles as a launch mechanism; runs on the operator's calendar |
| Paid live webinar / VIP session | ~$49 | Low-ticket live event that also functions as a launch |
| High-ticket program / mastermind | $2,000–$6,800+ | Backend offer; needs proof and pipeline first |
| Productized service / retainer | $1,500+/mo | The service-first path: pays now, manufactures product assets |
| AI-hybrid product (curriculum + encoded process: prompts, agents, skills, templates) | one-time or subscription — no settled band | **Emergent, few public receipts as of mid-2026.** The differentiated lane for operators whose asset is a process an AI can execute |

## 2026 signals (carry these into recommendations, with their confidence)

- **Implementation over information** — multiple independent sources converge on buyers paying for systems deployable in minutes over long video courses. Strong directional consensus.
- The widely-repeated "templates outsell courses 3:1" figure traces to a single low-authority source — **directional at best, never cite as fact.**
- **The free asset is engineered, not an afterthought** — the educational email course (a short, genuinely useful email sequence delivering a taste of the paid outcome, with the pitch at the end) is the documented standout, with one operator team attributing a multi-hundred-thousand-completion, revenue-quadrupling funnel to it (self-reported).
- **Portfolio iteration discipline** — the practitioners with portfolios judge products over multiple relaunches, kill on weak signal, and double down on strength. One launch is not a verdict.

## Fit filters (apply to every candidate / format decision)

**1. Platform stack — what `tools.md` actually supports.** Examples of the mapping (verify current fees at build time; they move):

- **Substack-centered stack:** no native one-off product sales — the three working patterns are (a) paid-post series as the product, (b) everything-behind-the-subscription with product drops as paid posts, (c) free list + external checkout (Gumroad / Stan / similar).
- **Gumroad / Stan / Whop-style stores:** fast checkout for one-off products. Fee structures differ and have changed recently (Gumroad's exact current fee was reported inconsistently across 2026 sources) — **check the pricing page the week you build, not from memory.**
- **Skool / Circle-style platforms:** community + course products; monthly platform cost means they only make sense above a revenue floor.
- **No commerce stack at all:** that's a finding — either the simplest possible store, or the service-first path (invoice, no storefront).

**2. Audience size.**

- **No list / tiny list:** waitlist-style validation can't clear its thresholds — the credible path is service-first (consult, get paid, harvest the artifacts) or building the free asset before the paid one.
- **Working list:** the waitlist test (defined in the scope skill's validation reference) is available and should gate any real build.

**3. Capacity mode — name it explicitly with the operator:**

- **Signature mode** (Clouse-style): one product that delivers the operator's core premise, iterated for years; everything else feeds it. Right when focus is scarce and the premise is clear.
- **Small-bets mode** (Vassallo-style): several timeboxed product bets, kill or double down on signal. Right when shipping velocity is high and the operator tolerates misses.
- Both are proven; drifting between them unconsciously is the failure mode.
