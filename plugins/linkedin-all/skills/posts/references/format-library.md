# Format library — skeletons, hooks, and 2026 platform reality

Distilled from the practitioners with the strongest public receipts as of mid-2026 — Justin Welsh (the systems-era foundation), Lara Acosta (the biggest current LinkedIn-craft creator), Matt Barker (the copy mechanic), Jasmin Alić (the engagement master) — cross-checked against the 2025 algorithm dataset (Richard van der Blom's report, 1.8M+ posts) and 2026 platform analyses. Frameworks are named and attributed; use the structures, never anyone's wording.

---

## Part 1 — Post skeletons

Every strong LinkedIn writer works inside named structures and reuses what wins. Pick the skeleton that fits the material; don't force material into a skeleton.

### Story → Lesson → Advice → You (Lara Acosta's SLAY)
Best for: personal-story posts — the format that builds a following.
1. **Story** — open inside a specific personal moment the ICP recognizes. Real details, real stakes.
2. **Lesson** — what it taught, stated plainly (heart → head).
3. **Advice** — the actionable version: concrete steps, not platitudes (→ hands).
4. **You** — turn it to the reader; invite their version of the story. (Real question, not engagement bait.)

### Problem → Agitate → Solution (PAS — Matt Barker runs it on nearly everything)
Best for: pain-point posts, positioning, capture posts.
1. Name the reader's problem precisely (their words, from `ICP.md`).
2. What it costs them — make the status quo uncomfortable.
3. The solution — mechanism + receipt. The capture/sale ask sits here if that's the post's job.
Rotations when PAS wears thin: BAB (Before → After → Bridge), AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action).

### The enemy opener (Justin Welsh's 3-step viral-post anatomy)
Best for: the first three lines of contrarian/observational posts. Three short lines, blank lines between:
1. **Scroll-stopper:** pick a relatable enemy and throw a rock — `<the enemy> is <what's happening to it>`.
2. **Flip:** champion the opposite hero — `<the hero> is <strong positive statement>`.
3. **Gasoline + open loop:** your emotional stake plus a question that pulls past "see more" — `And I <reaction>. <Why?/Here's what I mean:>`.
The "enemy" is a practice or norm, never a named person.

### Listicle
Best for: actionable posts, carousels. One promise in the hook ("N <things> that <outcome>"), N concrete items, each able to stand alone, no filler items to round the number up. Saves-friendly — close with "save this" only when it's genuinely reference material.

### X vs Y / Present vs Future / Contrarian (three of Welsh's eight Content Matrix styles)
Best for: positioning posts. Two columns of thinking — the common way vs the better way; where things are vs where they're going; what everyone believes vs what the evidence says. The contrarian take needs a receipt, or it's just contrarianism.

### Obvious problem, non-obvious solution (Matt Barker's viral pairing)
Best for: expertise display. A problem everyone in the ICP has + a fix they haven't heard + an image that illustrates it. The non-obvious part is the post; if the solution is obvious, it's not this skeleton.

### Story + related picture (Barker's other pairing; Lara's visual-first doctrine)
Best for: image posts. The photo is part of the hook — a real moment that makes the first line land harder. Roughly half of Lara Acosta's content is image posts; a real face raises engagement substantially (≈ +50% vs faceless images in van der Blom's 2025 data — directional, not a constant).

## Part 2 — Hooks

**The budget:** ~210 characters render above the fold (Welsh's figure); the first line should be ~8–10 words so it survives a phone screen (Lara's rule — most users are mobile). She puts "up to 80% of a post's success" on the hook; treat the exact number as her estimate, the priority as consensus.

**Hook types that work, with the mechanics:**

- **Curiosity gap** — promise a specific reveal: "I've never shared how <specific thing> actually happened."
- **Specific number / credential** — a real receipt doing the stopping: "I asked 100 customers why they paid me." (Real numbers only — a fabricated hook number is both a lie and, increasingly, a detectable pattern.)
- **Expectation break** — a sentence the reader didn't see coming: the hire made without an interview, the win that came from quitting.
- **Relatable enemy** — the Welsh opener above.
- **Hook-as-trailer** (Barker's compression process) — write the post first, then compress its payoff into one line: "How to <outcome> in <time> without <pain>." The hook summarizes the payoff; it never writes a check the post can't cash.

Process: draft the post, then write 3 hooks against it, then cut every needless word from the winner. Hooks are compressed last, not composed first.

## Part 3 — Format hierarchy (mid-2026 reality)

| Format | 2026 status | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| **Document / PDF carousel** | The engagement leader (~6.6% avg engagement, still climbing in 2026 datasets — directional) | Step-based or list content worth saving |
| **Text-only, ~700–1,000 chars** | The stable workhorse; majority of all content; engagement *up* in 2026 data | The default. Top performers: 16–20 short sentences, ≤4-line paragraphs, plain readability |
| **Image (real face) + text** | Face images ≈ +50% vs faceless (van der Blom) | Story posts; the image is part of the hook |
| **Short vertical video (<60–90s)** | Past its 2024–25 peak — video views down sharply YoY; platform quietly de-emphasizing | Cold-audience discovery only, and only if video already exists in the operation |
| **Poll** | Reputationally cringe; measurably the highest reach-multiplier on personal profiles in 2025 data (~1.6x) | A flagged experiment, never doctrine |

**Distribution mechanics that shape the draft:**

- **Golden hour is real:** the post is tested on a small slice of the network in the first ~60 minutes; a weak first hour rarely recovers. Fast, substantive replies to early comments (within ~15 minutes) extend distribution.
- **Weighting:** comments ≈ 2x likes; saves are weighted heavily (≈ 5 likes per save in AuthoredUp's data — directional). Build posts worth commenting on and worth saving.
- **Lifespan:** posts now surface for 2–3 weeks (dwell-time model) — evergreen-in-the-lane beats news-pegged for most operators.
- **Cadence:** 3–5 posts/week, never more than 1 per 24 hours. Consistency over 8+ weeks builds the profile's topical baseline.
- **Links:** external links in the body cost 40–60% reach. The first-comment workaround is reportedly detected too as of early 2026 (single source — probable, not proven). Honest options: no link (point at profile/Featured), or eat the penalty knowingly on a capture post.
- **The lane (360Brew era):** the ranking model matches post topic against profile + history semantically. Posts inside the declared lane compound authority; scattered topics structurally underperform. The question isn't "how do I get views" — it's "what topic should LinkedIn instantly associate with me."

## Part 4 — The swipe file (templatize your winners)

The shared doctrine (Welsh: analyze every winner, turn it into a template, reuse it; his current guidance extends it — write ~5 variations of a winner and stagger them over weeks/months. Barker keeps the same thing as a personal swipe file). Entry shape for `workspace/linkedin/swipe-file.md`:

```markdown
## <YYYY-MM-DD> — <template name, e.g. "enemy-opener → 3 receipts → question">
**Origin:** <piece folder path> — <result exactly as the operator reported it>
**Skeleton:**
- Hook move: <which hook type, abstracted>
- Beat 2: <...>
- Beat 3: <...>
- Close: <the move it ends on>
**Why it worked (best guess):** <one honest line>
**Variations run:** <dates + piece links as they happen>
```

Abstract the *structure*, drop the *content* — a template is reusable because the story slots out and a new one slots in.

## Part 5 — Dated practices: never ship these

- **Engagement pods** — actively detected (reciprocal patterns, timing clusters); documented reach collapses; LinkedIn's stated goal is making pods worthless.
- **Engagement bait** — "comment YES," reaction polls, tag-a-friend: pattern-detected and penalized.
- **Broetry** — one-line-per-sentence dramatic padding and empty platitudes; dwell-time ranking punishes it. Substance-dense short paragraphs won.
- **Hashtag strategy** — hashtag pages disabled since late 2024; zero-to-negative impact. 0–3 cosmetic max.
- **Creator Mode advice** — retired March 2024.
- **Generic AI output** — detected and suppressed (mechanism unverified across sources; direction consistent). The fix is the voice rule plus real receipts.
- **Cold DM blasts & automation** — automation is detected; automated comments get visibility-limited. Relationship-building happens in public comments first (see the plan skill's engagement system).
- **Chasing virality / follower counts** — the algorithm de-optimized for it, and the top practitioners treat LinkedIn as top-of-funnel into email anyway. The metric that matters is the operator's list and pipeline, not impressions.
