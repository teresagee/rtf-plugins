# The engagement system — comments as distribution

The strongest documented commenting system on LinkedIn is Jasmin Alić's — built 200K+ followers from Bosnia with zero starting network, runs on 100% inbound business, and was featured by LinkedIn itself. The principle: **comments are distribution, not aftercare.** Posting is optional; commenting is daily. This file distills the mechanics for the operator's strategy and daily routine. Everything here is done by the operator's own hands — never automated, never delegated to a tool.

---

## Why it works (2026 mechanics)

- A substantive comment puts the operator's name, face, and thinking in front of someone else's audience — borrowed distribution with zero follower requirement.
- The ranking model reads comment *depth* semantically: thoughtful comments in the lane build the same topical authority as posts. Comments weigh roughly double likes on the posts they sit on.
- Replying fast and substantively on your **own** posts (especially the first hour) extends the post's distribution window.
- Automation is the failure mode: LinkedIn detects automated/templated commenting and limits its visibility. The entire value is that a human wrote it.

## The target architecture

- **20–30 creators in the lane** + **~10 in adjacent lanes** (where the ICP also hangs out but competitors don't).
- Mix sizes: a few big accounts (reach), mostly mid-size active ones (visibility + actual relationships), a few peers (allies compound).
- Comment on people from the list **daily** — the routine survives on days the operator doesn't post.
- **Thread depth beats spread:** not one comment and gone — 3+ replies inside a good thread. Threads compound visibility; drive-bys don't.
- Prune quarterly: accounts that went quiet or drifted off-lane rotate out.

## The seven comment types (Alić's taxonomy, paraphrased)

Never "Great post." Every comment adds something only this operator could add:

1. **Personal take** — your actual position on the point, stated plainly.
2. **Personal advice** — "what I wish I'd known when I was doing this."
3. **Professional advice** — the thing you'd normally only tell clients.
4. **Restate sharper** — the post's idea in your own words, tightened (writers notice when someone compresses them well).
5. **Summarize to bullets** — distill a long post into its skeleton (high-value on carousels and essays).
6. **Respectful opposing view** — disagree with the idea, never the person, with a reason. The highest-signal comment type there is.
7. **Motivational / humor** — sparingly, only where it's natural; warmth is real value when it isn't filler.

Drafting support: the operator may paste a post into a session and ask for comment angles — give 2–3 in their voice (voice rule applies), as options to rewrite in their own hand, not scripts to paste blind. What makes the comment work is that it's genuinely theirs.

## On the operator's own posts

- **First hour is part of the post.** Be available; reply to every early comment within ~15 minutes where possible.
- **Replies carry value, not emojis.** A reply is a chance to add the second insight that didn't fit the post.
- **Good threads get continued.** When a conversation gets real, take it to DMs with the specific thing promised in the thread — this is how posts sell without selling.

## The DM stance (2026: inbound-led only)

The cold-DM era is over — DM blindness plus automation detection killed it. The working sequence:

1. **~5+ genuine public interactions first** (their posts, your posts, threads). The DM should never be the first touch.
2. **The opener:** reference something specific from their work or the thread + give one useful thing (a resource, an answer, the framework promised in the comments) + **no ask** + one open question.
3. **Then it's a conversation,** and business happens at conversation speed. No sequences, no scripts, no "just bumping this."

## Dated practices — never recommend these

- **Engagement pods** — reciprocal-engagement patterns are actively detected; documented reach collapses; LinkedIn's stated aim is making pods worthless.
- **Comment automation / AI auto-commenters** — detected; visibility of automated comments is limited by the platform.
- **Cold DM blasts and connection-request scripts** — dead channel, reputational cost on top.
- **Follow-for-follow, like-trains, "let's support each other" groups** — same detection family as pods, same outcome.
